Ideal Job

Cyber Security Analysts are responsible for the overall company’s systems and networks safety. They make sure networks and systems are protected from any malicious attacks. In this particular job ad, the responsibilities include monitoring and investigating any security incidents, conducting IT audits to ensure ongoing operational IT compliance, and participating in various security management processes. Working at an online security company for several years, I have learned how critical cybersecurity is. As more business go online these days, we see more crimes go online as well. For example, last year, under COVID, I saw more phishing attacks, and criminals are becoming better at deceiving people. As we are using more IT technologies in our daily lives, protecting our data and information is becoming more crucial. The importance of cybersecurity will only get bigger and bigger. Cyber Security Analysts will protect people and make the cyber world secure and safe, which makes this career appealing to me.

To be a Cyber Security Analyst, you are required to have a Bachelor's degree in an IT-related field, such as information technology, computer science, or cybersecurity. Internship experience in the cybersecurity area would help, but one can also start his or her career in any IT industry area and move up to a cybersecurity role to obtain broad experience in various areas. Analysing and investigating all security events in systems and networks, one would need to possess a broad understating of a range of IT technologies. Not only soft skills but some technical skills will be required, including Network Security, Information systems, Cryptography, Python and Vulnerability assessment. Some certifications would be beneficial, such as CISSP (A Certified Information Systems Security Professional) and CISA (A Certified Information Systems Auditor). Other general skills are also required, such as communication, problem-solving, organisation, and teamwork skills. These skills will be essential in working in a group and interacting with the management team or clients.
